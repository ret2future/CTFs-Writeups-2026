EXPLAIN LIKE I'M 5: WHY MY MATH WAS WRONG
==========================================

Imagine you have 120 toys and you need to sort them into 3 boxes:
- MINOR box (for small toys)
- MEDIUM box (for medium toys) 
- MAJOR box (for big toys)

Your friend tells you which box each toy REALLY goes in (the "known" box).
You try to guess which box each toy goes in (the "detected" box).

FALSE POSITIVE = You said "this toy goes in this box" but you were WRONG
FALSE NEGATIVE = You said "this toy does NOT go in this box" but you were WRONG

MY MISTAKE:
I was counting ALL the toys for EACH box separately. 

Like this:
For the MINOR box, I looked at ALL 120 toys and counted:
- How many times I said "minor" when it wasn't really minor (FP)
- How many times I said "not minor" when it was really minor (FN)

But that's wrong! I should only count the toys that could actually go in that box.

THE RIGHT WAY:
For the MINOR box, I should only look at the toys that are EITHER:
- Really minor, OR
- I said were minor

Let me show you with just 10 toys:

Toy 1: Really minor, I said minor = CORRECT
Toy 2: Really major, I said major = correct for major, but doesn't matter for minor
Toy 5: Really major, I said medium = this matters for MAJOR box (FN) and MEDIUM box (FP)
Toy 8: Really medium, I said minor = this matters for MINOR box (FP) and MEDIUM box (FN)

See? Each toy can affect MULTIPLE boxes!

So when I calculate:
False Positive Rate for MINOR = (times I said minor but it wasn't) / (times I said minor + times it wasn't minor and I didn't say minor)

But I was dividing by ALL 120 toys, not just the relevant ones!

That's like asking "What percentage of ALL toys did I get wrong for the minor box?"
instead of 
"What percentage of toys that could be minor did I get wrong?"

It's the difference between:
- "How good am I at sorting toys overall?" (WRONG question)
- "How good am I at telling if a toy is minor or not?" (RIGHT question)

So my numbers were wrong because I was using the wrong denominator!
I was dividing by 120 instead of the actual number of relevant toys for each classification.
